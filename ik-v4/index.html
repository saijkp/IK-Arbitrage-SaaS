Common questions

Everything UK buyers ask
before their first order

Do I need to know exactly what I want before contacting you?+
No — and most of our clients don't. A mood board, a photo of something you've seen, or even just a description is enough to start. We ask the right questions and come back with options within 48–72 hours.
What's the minimum order?+
There's no formal minimum, but orders under around £2,000 landed value are usually uneconomical due to fixed freight costs. We'll always tell you honestly if an order doesn't make financial sense, and we can often consolidate small orders with others to share freight costs.
How do you ensure quality when I can't visit the factory?+
Three ways: we source from factories we have established relationships with; we offer physical samples shipped to your UK address before bulk production; and we conduct a pre-shipment inspection of every order with a full photo report shared with you before the container seals.
Do your products meet UK fire safety and compliance standards?+
Yes. For upholstered furniture we ensure BS 7177 fire resistance compliance. For electrical items we provide UKCA documentation. Every order includes a full compliance pack — test certificates, material specs, country of origin. Ready to drop straight into your project file.
What do you actually charge?+
A sourcing and QC fee of 5% of product value (minimum £199), included in every landed cost quote. The price you see is the all-in price: product, freight, UK customs clearance, import duty, our fee, and last-mile delivery. No hidden charges. Use our cost calculator to see a full breakdown for your product.
How long from order to site?+
Typically 10–12 weeks door-to-site: 3–5 weeks production + 5 weeks sea transit + 1 week UK customs and delivery. For urgent orders we can use air freight (5–10 days transit, higher cost). We quote a specific confirmed delivery date and stand behind it.
Can you handle phased delivery for a multi-block development?+
Yes — this is a speciality. One consolidated order, staggered shipping timed to your handover programme. Block A in April, Block B in July, for example. We manage the warehousing split in China so you coordinate nothing.
